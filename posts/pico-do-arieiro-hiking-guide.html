Pico do Arieiro: The Complete 2026 Guide

9 July 20267 min readFunchal · Madeira

At 1,818 metres, Pico do Arieiro is Madeira's third-highest peak and the easiest to reach — a paved road runs almost to the summit, which is why it's also the island's most-photographed sunrise. Here's what's changed for 2026, what it costs, and how to do it properly.

Do you need to book to visit Pico do Arieiro?

Only if you're hiking the trail beyond the summit. The viewpoint at Pico do Arieiro — the car park, the miradouro and the short paved path around it — is free, unbooked and open around the clock, which is why it's the default sunrise spot for most visitors. What does require a reservation is the PR1 trail, the famous ridge walk on to Pico Ruivo, Madeira's highest peak. After being closed for most of 2025 following wildfire damage, PR1 fully reopened on 1 May 2026 under the island's new trail management system: every hiker now needs a booked 30-minute entry slot through the official SIMplifica platform before setting out. There's no paying at the trailhead and no walk-up access — book online in advance.

How much does the Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo hike cost?

The PR1 trail costs €10.50 per person, paid at the time of booking on SIMplifica. Residents of the Autonomous Region of Madeira hike for free once they've verified their status with a resident or citizen card, and children aged 12 and under are exempt from the fee entirely — though both groups still need to complete the registration and reserve a time slot like everyone else. Book a few days ahead in peak season (June to September), since popular sunrise slots sell out.

When should you go, and is the sunrise worth it?

Yes — it's one of the best free experiences on the island, but the timing is unforgiving. Aim to arrive at the car park 45 to 60 minutes before official sunrise, both to catch the light building over the sea of clouds below the peak and because parking is limited and fills up fast with tour minibuses and rental cars alike. You don't need to book anything to watch the sunrise from the viewpoint itself; the SIMplifica reservation is only needed if you're continuing onto the PR1 trail afterwards. Bring a torch for the walk from the car park in the dark, and expect temperatures well below what you left in Funchal.

How hard is the PR1 trail to Pico Ruivo?

It's a moderate but serious mountain hike, not a stroll — roughly 7 kilometres one-way with around 500 metres of combined ascent and descent along an exposed volcanic ridge. Since the 2026 reopening, PR1 runs one-way only, from Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo; hiking it in reverse is no longer permitted, so plan your return via the alternative descent routes or arrange a taxi from the Achada do Teixeira car park near Pico Ruivo. Along the way you'll pass through four or five short, unlit volcanic tunnels — originally cut for shepherds and cattle — and cross narrow sections of ridge with sheer drops on both sides.

Is the PR1 trail safe for people afraid of heights?

Cable railings and stone stairways protect most of the exposed sections, and the path itself is well maintained, but the drops either side of the ridge are real and the tunnels are pitch-dark without your own light. It's not the trail to attempt if you have severe vertigo, and it should be avoided entirely in strong wind or heavy rain, when the exposed ridge sections become genuinely dangerous. If in doubt, the summit viewpoint at Pico do Arieiro alone — no trail required — already delivers the panorama most visitors actually came for.

What should you pack for Pico do Arieiro?

Bring layers — it's routinely 10°C or more colder at 1,818 metres than down in Funchal, and wind chill at the summit can be sharp even in summer. A head torch is essential for the tunnels and for a pre-dawn arrival, along with sturdy closed shoes, water, and your SIMplifica booking confirmation saved on your phone in case signal drops. Mountain weather here changes quickly, so check the forecast the same morning rather than the night before — cloud can roll in and erase the view within minutes.

How does Pico do Arieiro fit into a Madeira trip?

Most visitors treat it as a half-day: sunrise at the summit, breakfast back in Funchal, and the rest of the day free. It pairs naturally with a very different kind of Madeira morning — swapping the mountain's cold, thin air for a warm afternoon at sea level. After an early start on the peaks, plenty of our guests come straight down to the marina for a private boat trip with Chifbay, trading cliffside views for the water's-eye version of the same island.

The road takes you to the summit. The trail takes you to the ridge. Either way, Madeira looks different from up there.

Whichever version you choose — a free sunrise at the viewpoint, or the full booked hike to Pico Ruivo — go early, dress warm, and check the trail status before you drive up.
